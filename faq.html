Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about these firearms

What does "semi-automatic" mean?

A semi-automatic firearm fires one round per trigger pull and automatically chambers the next round. This is different from a fully automatic weapon, which fires continuously as long as the trigger is held.

Are these firearms legal?

Firearm laws vary by state and locality. The firearms featured on this site are legal in many states for qualified owners who meet licensing and background check requirements. Always check your local laws before purchasing.

What is "caliber"?

Caliber refers to the diameter of the bullet measured in millimeters or inches. It's one of the key specifications that determines the power and performance of a firearm. Different calibers are used for different purposes.

What is safe firearm ownership?

Safe firearm ownership includes proper training, secure storage, regular maintenance, and responsible handling. Always follow local laws, take certified safety courses, and store firearms securely away from unauthorized users.

Why are these firearms popular?

These three firearms are among the most owned in America due to their reliability, proven performance, reasonable cost, and versatility. They're popular for self-defense, sport shooting, hunting, and recreational use.

Can I modify these firearms?

Many firearms can be customized or modified, but this depends on local laws and regulations. Always ensure any modifications comply with local, state, and federal laws. The AR-15 is particularly known for its modular design and customization options.
